HSM Deployment — Government-Owned Financial Institution

A state-owned financial institution operating under central bank oversight required Hardware Security Module infrastructure to satisfy regulatory cryptographic requirements, improve key management practices, and establish audit-ready documentation for compliance review. The engagement involved a formal public procurement process, multiple vendor stakeholders, and strict approval timelines tied to regulatory deadlines.

Context & Challenge

The institution lacked a structured cryptographic governance framework. Vendor proposals varied significantly in scope interpretation, and internal stakeholders had limited baseline to evaluate technical claims. The procurement process required documentation that satisfied both regulatory and internal audit standards simultaneously.

My Role

Coordinated alignment between the vendor team, client technical staff, and institutional decision-makers. Ensured that solution scope was documented accurately against regulatory expectations. Managed the review cycle for compliance documentation, reducing revision loops by surfacing ambiguities before formal submission.

Outcome

Regulatory documentation approved without major revision. Delivery achieved within the compliance milestone window. The institution established a documented cryptographic governance posture aligned with central bank requirements.

Financial Services Network Security · Service Availability

Anti-DDoS Solution Evaluation — Tier-1 Commercial Bank

A large commercial bank had experienced service disruptions from volumetric and application-layer denial-of-service activity. With regulatory uptime obligations and customer-facing service continuity at stake, senior leadership required a structured evaluation process that could produce a defensible procurement decision — not just a technical recommendation.

Context & Challenge

Leadership had received conflicting vendor claims without a framework to evaluate them. The gap between technical incident language and business risk language was creating decision paralysis. The procurement timeline was time-sensitive due to an upcoming regulatory review cycle.

My Role

Reframed the evaluation from a technical comparison to a business risk discussion. Structured the vendor matrix around availability impact, total cost of ownership, and regulatory alignment — rather than feature specifications. Prepared executive briefing materials that supported clear decision-making at the board level.

Outcome

Decision clarity achieved within the procurement window. Leadership selected a solution with documented rationale across business impact, cost, and compliance criteria. Procurement formalized with appropriate documentation for regulatory audit purposes.

Skills Demonstrated

DDoS / Network Security Business Risk Translation Vendor Evaluation Executive Communication Procurement Documentation
